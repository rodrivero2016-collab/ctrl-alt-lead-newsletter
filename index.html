The Big Picture

Leadership Isn't About Strategy Anymore: It's About Humanity

As AI reshapes every workflow and uncertainty becomes the new normal, leaders face an almost impossible task: combine tech savvy with empathy, all while juggling a thousand other daily fires. So buckle up!

Here's the truth: In 2026, technical skills might get you invited to the leadership table, but human skills are what keep you there.

Why This Matters Right Now

AI isn't just a shiny new tool anymore: it's embedded in everyday workflows, reshaping how we make decisions. Leaders can't just adopt AI blindly; they need AI discernment. That means knowing when to trust the algorithm and when human judgment absolutely matters more.

Plus, organizations are going flatter. Leadership now operates more on influence than authority. And there's a growing gap between how fast things are changing and how prepared leaders actually are to handle it all.

Translation? You're being asked to do more, faster, with fewer safety nets. No pressure, right?

But Here's Why Leaders Struggle With It

Most leaders were trained to deliver answers, not ask questions. But in today's world, asking the right questions, and being okay with saying "I don't know," is actually a leadership strength.

The best leaders? They're the best students. They want to learn and understand the problem deeply.

Real Talk: My Ticket Software Transition

I once had to transition my team from Excel to a new ticket software for client issues. First week? Only ONE person used it. I could've demanded compliance through the team lead (spoiler: that wasn't working). Instead, I sent a team email celebrating that one person for leading the charge. Next week, three people used it. Then more. Several weeks later, everyone had transitioned. Leading with empathy and recognition worked where authority couldn't.

The Question Game

I've developed what I call "the question game" for myself. When guiding staff, I force myself to only use questions to lead them to the answer. It requires patience and careful thought about the resolution I want, but it's way better than dictating steps because it puts ownership back on the team member. Try it; it changes everything.
